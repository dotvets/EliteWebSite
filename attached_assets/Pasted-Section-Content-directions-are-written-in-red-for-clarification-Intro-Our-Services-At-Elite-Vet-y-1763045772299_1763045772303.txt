Our Veterinary Diagnostic Services Include: Laboratory Tests Laboratory tests are essential for diagnosing a variety of health conditions. Our in-house laboratory allows us to quickly and accurately analyze blood, urine, and fecal samples. Blood Tests: ● Complete Blood Count (CBC): A CBC provides a comprehensive evaluation of your pet's overall health, including red blood cell count, white blood cell count, platelet count, and hematocrit. This test can help identify anemia, infection, inflammation, and other health issues. ● Blood Chemistry Profile: This test measures levels of various substances in the blood, such as glucose, electrolytes, liver enzymes, and kidney function tests. It helps assess organ function, identify metabolic disorders, and monitor the effects of medications. ● Thyroid Function Tests: These tests measure thyroid hormone levels to diagnose conditions like hypothyroidism and hyperthyroidism, which can affect your pet's metabolism, energy levels, and overall health. ● Heartworm Tests: Heartworm disease is a serious parasitic disease that can affect dogs. This test detects the presence of heartworm parasites in your pet's blood. ● PCW Tests: A comprehensive blood test that provides a detailed look into your pet's overall health. It involves a combination of tests that assess various organ functions and identify potential health issues early on. This test can help identify conditions such as kidney disease, liver disease, diabetes, and other health problems. Urine & Fecal Tests ● Urinalysis: This test analyzes the physical, chemical, and microscopic properties of urine to detect infections, kidney disease, liver disease, and other health problems. ● Urine Culture and Sensitivity: This test identifies bacteria causing urinary tract infections and determines the most effective antibiotic treatment. ● Fecal Parasite Examination: This test detects intestinal parasites such as roundworms, hookworms, whipworms, and coccidia. ● Fecal Bacteria Culture and Sensitivity: This test identifies bacteria causing gastrointestinal infections and determines the most effective antibiotic treatment. X-rays X-rays are used to diagnose a variety of conditions, including bone fractures, joint diseases, foreign body ingestion, respiratory diseases, and urinary tract diseases. Ultrasound Ultrasound imaging uses sound waves to create images of internal organs, such as the heart, liver, kidneys, spleen, and bladder. It can help diagnose conditions like heart disease, kidney disease, liver disease, pregnancy, and abdominal masses. Endoscopy A minimally invasive procedure that allows our experts to examine the internal organs of your pet. It involves inserting a flexible tube with a tiny camera into the body to visualize the digestive, respiratory, or urinary tract. This procedure can help diagnose conditions such as ulcers, inflammation, polyps, tumors, and foreign body ingestion. CT Scan CT scans provide detailed cross-sectional images of your pet's body, allowing us to diagnose complex conditions such as brain tumors, spinal cord injuries, internal organ masses, and bone fractures.
